============================================================
KEY FINDINGS
============================================================

1. MES responds to VERY TIGHT Keltner channels (mult 0.5-0.75) unlike MGC which
   used 1.25-1.5. This makes sense — S&P has tighter ranges and more frequent
   breakouts than gold.

2. The S&P REQUIRES both long+short trading. Long-only strategies all lost money
   due to the significant selloffs in the data period. Gold was long-biased.

3. Win rate is lower (~53%) but profit factor is MUCH higher (4.5 vs 2.5) because
   the strategy lets winners run (2.0R TP) while cutting losers fast (1.5x ATR trail).
   The avg winner/loser ratio of 3.4:1 is excellent.

4. ADX filter has NO effect on MES — the best strategies all performed identically
   with ADX>0, >5, and >10. Can be removed.

5. Strategies that FAILED on MES:
   - MA Ribbon, Dual MA Crossover, Momentum ROC — all massive losers
   - Fibonacci Pullback — poor results  
   - Volatility Contraction — too few signals
   - Higher Highs pattern — negative expectancy

6. P&L comparison (annualized):
   - MGC Keltner: ~$8,463/yr (2yr: $16,926)
   - MES Keltner: ~$38,819/yr (1yr data — needs more history to confirm)
   - MES generates ~4.6x the P&L but with lower win rate

RECOMMENDATION:
  The MES Keltner KC(14, 0.5) strategy is very promising but needs validation
  on more data. The tight channel + wide TP approach is a different style than
  MGC (high WR vs high R:R). Consider paper trading to validate before live.
